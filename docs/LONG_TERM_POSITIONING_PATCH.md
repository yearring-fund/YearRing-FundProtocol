# Long-Term Positioning Patch

## Why a 30–365 Day Lock Still Supports a Long-Horizon System

FinancialBase should not be described as a long-term system simply because it supports locks of up to 365 days.

A one-year lock, by itself, is better understood as a medium-horizon commitment. What makes FinancialBase long-horizon is the broader architecture built around that commitment layer:

1. **Yield exists independently of token incentives.** The vault generates base yield through strategy performance. Users do not need the token to access that underlying yield.
2. **Commitment is rewarded, and early exit remains accountable.** The system does not treat locking as a cosmetic APY multiplier. It treats it as a structured commitment with explicit incentives and explicit unwind conditions.
3. **Positions are designed for continuity rather than short-term churn.** The architecture is built to preserve long-duration positions and reduce the fragility that normally comes with purely opportunistic liquidity.
4. **The lock window functions as a repeatable commitment primitive.** A 30–365 day lock is not presented as the full definition of long-term capital. It is the first on-chain commitment layer in a broader system designed to favor capital that stays, compounds, and remains aligned over time.

In other words, the current lock range should not be interpreted as the entire long-term story. The long-term character of FinancialBase comes from the combination of an asset-management base, commitment incentives, early-exit accountability, and continuity design.

## Why This Is Not Just Lock-Up Liquidity Mining

FinancialBase should also be distinguished from a lock-up farming scheme.

In a typical liquidity mining model, token emissions are the primary reason users stay. In FinancialBase, the base yield comes from the vault and its strategy layer. The commitment layer sits on top of that yield base rather than replacing it.

This means the lock mechanism is not designed to manufacture yield through token issuance. It is designed to coordinate user behavior around longer holding periods, lower churn, and clearer commitment. The token does not create the floor of the system. The vault does.

That is why the current design should be understood as a **long-horizon asset architecture**, not merely a short-term vault with a lock-up switch.
